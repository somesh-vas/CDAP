WELCOME TO CELL FOR DIFFERENTLY ABLE PERSONS

Cell for Differently Abled Person (CDAP) was established on 14 February 2017. Efforts have been made to make education and the stay on campus more inclusive for the differently-abled students. The differently abled students in higher educational institutes such as IITs face hindrances in their personal and professional growth due to some limitations in utilizing the rich cultural and educational environment provided by the institutes. IIT Kanpur is committed to Inclusive Education for all students on campus, and CDAP aims to be the stepping-stone for the process.

"Being disabled does not mean Un-abled, just Different Abled."
- Mr. Noel Helm

Academic Accommodations

Accommodations in Class

1 Special tutors may be assigned for students to assist after class hours.
2 Seating preferences in class for better accessibility.
3 Laboratory assistance based on individual needs.
4 Accessible format as in slides, permission to record lectures etc may be requested to faculty.

Accommodations in Examinations

1 Reader/scribe may be provided.
2 Raised chair/table may be provided.
3 Accessible format of question paper as in preferred fonts or font size.
4 Extended time as directed by Government of India regulations

Accessibility Survey

An access audit is a form of inspection that can be used to assess the ease of access to, and ease of use of; an environment (such as a building or landscape), a service, or a facility, by people with a range of access impairments. As a first step towards access audit in campus, with the approval of Director IITK, Accessibility Survey was held for IITK buildings (Existing/ under construction/ Planned). The responsibility for the same was entrusted to Prof. Gaurav Raheja, IIT Roorkee (Expert in Accessibility Standards and guidelines appointed by institute). His views were sought on implementation of barrier free design in all buildings on campus

Participation in Inclusive University Alliance



IIT Kanpur is partnering country-wide with likeminded institutes under IUA (Inclusive University Alliance). IUA is a group of institutes of higher education and organizations working on accessibility of STEM education for persons with disabilities. Participation in IUA provides an opportunity for IIT Kanpur to keep connected with other institute agencies working in similar areas and sharing our knowledge pool and resources.

Health Camp

By 1980 batch

The 1980 batch of IIT Kanpur organized a free diagnosis and counselling health camp for differently abled people at the health centre IIT Kanpur. In collaboration with ALIMCO and DORA, CDAP helped spread information about the camp and handled the logistics.
The event is the first event of its kind at IIT Kanpur Campus and benefitted many Differently Abled people, including students/staff/faculty. During the distribution session held shortly after the camp, assistive devices, including wheelchairs, were distributed to some of those, who were examined at the camp.
